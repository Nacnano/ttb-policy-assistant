# Social Media Policy

## Overview

This policy governs how TTB Bank employees use social media, both on behalf of the bank and in a personal capacity. It applies to all forms of social media including Facebook, LinkedIn, X (Twitter), Instagram, TikTok, blogs, and forums.

## Official Bank Accounts

Only authorised employees in the Corporate Communications department may post on behalf of TTB Bank on official social media channels. All content must be approved by the Head of Corporate Communications before publication.

## Personal Social Media Use

Employees are free to use personal social media accounts but must observe the following rules:

### Prohibited Content

- **Disclosure of bank strategy, products, or plans** that have not been publicly announced
- Confidential customer information, transaction data, or internal operational details
- Content that is defamatory, discriminatory, or harassing towards colleagues or customers
- Negative comments about competitors that could expose the bank to legal liability
- Insider information about the bank's financial performance

### Required Disclaimer

When expressing personal opinions on topics related to banking, finance, or TTB Bank, employees must include a clear disclaimer such as:

> "Views expressed are my own and do not represent those of TMBThanachart Bank."

### Identification as Employee

Employees who identify themselves as TTB Bank employees on social media must behave in a professional manner that reflects positively on the bank.

## Photography and Video

Employees must not photograph or film bank premises, colleagues, customers, or bank systems without explicit authorisation.

## Monitoring

The bank may monitor publicly available social media content. Employees should have no expectation of privacy on public platforms.

## Consequences

Violations may result in disciplinary action, including termination. Disclosure of confidential information may also result in legal action.
