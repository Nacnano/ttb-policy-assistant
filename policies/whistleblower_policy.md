# Whistleblower Policy

## Overview

TTB Bank encourages employees and third parties to report suspected wrongdoing, misconduct, or policy violations without fear of retaliation. This policy establishes a safe, confidential channel for raising concerns.

## What Can Be Reported

The whistleblower channel may be used to report:

- Fraud, bribery, or corruption
- Violations of law or regulations
- Significant financial irregularities
- Serious health and safety breaches
- Harassment or discrimination that management has failed to address
- Cover-ups or destruction of evidence

## Reporting Channels

Concerns may be raised through:

1. **Anonymous hotline**: 1800-XXX-XXXX (available 24/7, operated by an independent third party)
2. **Ethics email**: ethics@ttb.co.th (can be submitted anonymously via web form)
3. **Audit Committee**: For concerns involving senior management, reports may be sent directly to the Audit Committee Chair

## Anonymity

Reports may be submitted anonymously. The bank will not attempt to identify anonymous reporters unless required by law. Anonymous reports will still be investigated, though the ability to investigate may be limited if insufficient information is provided.

## Investigation Process

1. All reports are acknowledged within **3 working days**
2. A preliminary assessment is completed within **10 working days**
3. Full investigations are completed within **30 calendar days** (may be extended for complex cases)
4. The reporter (if identifiable) is informed of the outcome

## Non-Retaliation

Any form of retaliation against an employee who raises a concern in good faith is a serious disciplinary offence and may be grounds for immediate termination. Retaliation includes demotion, harassment, exclusion, or any adverse employment action.

## False Reports

Deliberately making a false or malicious report is a disciplinary offence. However, reporters acting in good faith will not be penalised even if the concern is not upheld following investigation.
