# Know Your Customer (KYC) Policy Summary

## Overview

TTB Bank is required by Thai law and the Bank of Thailand (BOT) regulations to conduct Know Your Customer (KYC) and Customer Due Diligence (CDD) checks on all customers. This document summarises the KYC framework for operational staff.

## CDD Tiers

### Standard CDD

Applied to all retail customers and low-risk business customers. Includes:

- Verification of identity (national ID, passport)
- Verification of address
- Collection of occupation and source of funds information

### Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Applied to higher-risk customers including:

- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their immediate family members
- Non-resident customers
- High-value cash transaction customers (above ฿2 million per transaction)
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions (as listed by FATF)

EDD requires approval from the Compliance Officer and documented rationale for onboarding.

## Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

All PEPs and their associates must undergo Enhanced Due Diligence regardless of transaction amounts. PEP status must be checked against the bank's approved screening tool at onboarding and at every annual review.

## Review Cycles

Customer profiles must be reviewed on the following schedule:

| Risk Level | Review Frequency |
|---|---|
| Low Risk | Every 3 years |
| Medium Risk | Every 2 years |
| High Risk (incl. PEPs) | Every 1 year |

## Suspicious Transaction Reporting

Any customer activity that appears suspicious must be reported to the AML/CFT team within **24 hours** of detection. Employees must not tip off customers about investigations.

## Failure to Complete KYC

Accounts may not be opened if KYC documentation is incomplete. Existing accounts with expired KYC documentation must be restricted pending renewal.

## Contact

For KYC escalations, contact the Compliance Team at compliance@ttb.co.th.
