# Code of Conduct Policy

## Overview

TTB Bank expects all employees to act with the highest standards of integrity, professionalism, and ethical conduct. This Code of Conduct applies to all TTB employees, contractors, and agents.

## Conflicts of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with the bank's interests. All potential conflicts of interest must be disclosed to the employee's manager and the Compliance team.

Examples requiring disclosure:

- Owning a significant stake in a company that is or may become a bank customer or supplier
- Family members employed at competitor banks in senior roles
- Personal relationships with bank customers that could influence business decisions

## Gifts and Entertainment

- Employees may accept gifts worth up to **฿3,000** from external parties (clients, vendors) in a calendar year.
- Gifts exceeding **฿3,000** must be **declined or surrendered** to the bank and reported to Compliance.
- Giving gifts to customers or government officials in excess of ฿3,000 is prohibited (see Anti-Bribery Policy).
- All gifts received must be recorded in the Gifts and Hospitality register.

## Personal Trading

Employees with access to material non-public information (MNPI) must not trade in the securities of any company about which they hold MNPI. This includes TTB Bank's own shares. Pre-clearance is required for all trades in TTB shares by employees in designated roles.

## Reporting Misconduct

Employees who witness or suspect misconduct must report it via:

- Direct manager (if not implicated)
- The Compliance team (compliance@ttb.co.th)
- The anonymous whistleblower hotline (see Whistleblower Policy)

Retaliation against employees who report in good faith is strictly prohibited.

## Social Media and Public Statements

Employees must not make public statements on behalf of the bank without prior authorisation from Corporate Communications. Personal opinions shared on social media must include a disclaimer that views are personal and not those of TTB Bank.

## Consequences

Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action, including termination and referral to regulatory or law enforcement authorities.
