# Performance Review Policy

## Overview

TTB Bank uses a structured performance management process to set clear expectations, provide ongoing feedback, and recognise and develop employee talent.

## Review Cycle

| Review | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Annual Review | December each year | Full performance and compensation review |
| Mid-Year Review | June each year | Progress check; update goals |
| Probation Review | Month 2 of probation | Early performance assessment |

## Rating Scale

Performance is rated on a 5-point scale:

| Rating | Description |
|---|---|
| 5 – Outstanding | Consistently exceeds all objectives with exceptional impact |
| 4 – Exceeds Expectations | Meets all and exceeds most objectives |
| 3 – Meets Expectations | Fully meets all objectives |
| 2 – Below Expectations | Partially meets objectives; improvement required |
| 1 – Unsatisfactory | Fails to meet most objectives |

## Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

Employees who receive a rating of **2 or below** at the annual review must be placed on a **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)**:

- PIP duration: 90 days
- PIP includes specific, measurable improvement targets and weekly check-ins with manager
- Failure to meet PIP targets may result in termination

## Calibration

All ratings are subject to a calibration process led by HR to ensure consistency across teams. Managers must be prepared to justify ratings above 4 or below 2.

## Compensation Link

Annual salary increases and bonuses are linked to performance ratings:

| Rating | Typical Salary Increase | Bonus Eligibility |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | 8–12% | 100–150% of target |
| 4 | 5–8% | 80–100% of target |
| 3 | 3–5% | 60–80% of target |
| 2 | 0% | None |
| 1 | 0% | None; PIP mandatory |

## Goal Setting

Goals must be set using the SMART framework at the start of each year and agreed between employee and manager. Goals may be updated at mid-year with mutual agreement.
