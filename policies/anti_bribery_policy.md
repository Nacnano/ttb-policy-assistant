# Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy

## Overview

TTB Bank has a **zero-tolerance policy** towards bribery and corruption in all forms. This policy applies to all employees, directors, agents, and third parties acting on behalf of the bank, whether in Thailand or internationally.

## What Constitutes Bribery

Bribery is the offering, giving, receiving, or soliciting of anything of value to influence the actions of an official or other person in a position of authority. This includes:

- Cash payments
- Gifts, hospitality, or entertainment beyond permitted thresholds (see Code of Conduct)
- Loans, kickbacks, or favours
- Donations made with an expectation of preferential treatment

## Facilitating Payments

**Facilitating payments** — small payments made to expedite routine government actions — are **prohibited** regardless of local custom or competitive pressure.

## Government Officials

Special care must be taken when interacting with government officials. Any gift, meal, or entertainment provided to a government official must be pre-approved by Compliance, even if the value is below standard thresholds.

## Third-Party Due Diligence

Before engaging agents, consultants, or business partners, TTB Bank must conduct anti-bribery due diligence. Contracts with third parties must include anti-bribery representations and audit rights.

## Reporting Suspected Bribery

Any employee who suspects bribery or corruption must report it immediately to:

- The Compliance team (compliance@ttb.co.th)
- The anonymous whistleblower hotline

Failure to report known or suspected bribery is itself a disciplinary offence.

## Non-Retaliation

Employees who report bribery concerns in good faith are protected from retaliation.

## Recordkeeping

All hospitality, gifts, and political donations must be accurately recorded. Falsifying records to conceal payments is a serious disciplinary and criminal offence.

## Consequences

Violations may result in immediate termination and referral to the Royal Thai Police or relevant regulatory authorities.
