# IT Acceptable Use Policy

## Overview

This policy defines the acceptable use of TTB Bank's information technology resources, including computers, networks, software, and communications systems. All employees, contractors, and third-party users with access to bank IT systems must comply.

## Permitted Uses

IT resources may be used for:

- Performing job responsibilities and bank-related tasks
- Approved training and professional development
- Limited personal use that does not interfere with work duties

## Prohibited Activities

The following are strictly prohibited:

- Installing **personal or unauthorised software** on bank-owned devices
- Connecting **USB drives or external storage devices** without IT Security approval
- Accessing or storing bank data on personal cloud services (e.g., personal Google Drive, Dropbox)
- Bypassing network security controls, firewalls, or content filters
- Using bank systems for personal commercial activities or cryptocurrency mining
- Sharing login credentials with colleagues or third parties

## Remote Access

- Employees must connect via the **approved VPN** when accessing bank systems from outside office premises.
- Use of public Wi-Fi without VPN is prohibited.
- VPN credentials must not be shared.

## Software Installation

Only software approved by the IT department may be installed on bank devices. Requests for new software must be submitted via the IT helpdesk and approved by the IT Security team.

## Monitoring

TTB Bank reserves the right to monitor all activity on bank IT systems, including emails, internet usage, and file transfers. Employees have no expectation of privacy when using bank IT resources.

## Incident Reporting

Any suspected security incident, data loss, or malware infection must be reported to the IT Security team immediately via the helpdesk at itsecurity@ttb.co.th or ext. 5555.

## Consequences

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.
